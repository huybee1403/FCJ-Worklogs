## 2. The Highlights Worth Cherishing

### 🎯 Real-World Experience & Trust (Empowerment)
What I cherish most at FCAJ was not the scale of the projects, but the **trust and empowerment**. Contrary to the stereotype that "interns only do busywork," here I was directly responsible for important tasks. A prime example was **designing and deploying the LMS-Management-System myself** — an online learning management platform running on a highly available AWS architecture (VPC, EC2, Application Load Balancer, Auto Scaling, Amazon DocumentDB Multi-AZ, AWS Amplify, along with a CloudWatch/CloudTrail/SNS monitoring suite). Seeing the system I built run smoothly and scale automatically with the load was an incredibly powerful source of motivation.

### 👥 A Culture of Sharing & Support (Mentorship & Community)
- **High-quality mentorship:** The mentors did not "hold your hand," but instead guided a way of thinking (troubleshooting) so that I could find solutions on my own. This approach helped me tremendously develop my independence and problem-solving skills.
- **A dedicated Admin team:** Always listening, staying close, and creating the best conditions for interns to focus on their expertise.
- **An open community:** An environment that encourages technical debate and knowledge sharing, with no gap between newcomers and experts. I also had the chance to broaden my perspective through **4 technology events** (AWS Community Day Vietnam, the "Hera" Voice AI Agent Workshop, AWS Student Builder Group - Kiro Spec Driven Development, and FCAJ - HUTECH Kickoff).

### 📚 A Well-Structured Training Roadmap
The roadmap, moving from fundamentals (Compute, Networking, Storage) to advanced topics (Serverless, IaC, CI/CD, Monitoring), was designed with impeccable logic. The labs closely mirrored real business scenarios, helping to close the gap between academic theory and the real working environment, while also laying the groundwork for me to apply everything directly to the LMS project.

### ✍️ Honing Technical Writing & Sharing Skills
Writing and publishing **3 technical blogs** helped me solidify my knowledge and practice my presentation skills:
1. Building a multi-tenant configuration system with tagged storage patterns.
2. Securely connecting the AWS DevOps Agent to private services within a VPC.
3. Proactively monitoring Amazon Redshift Serverless with AWS Lambda and Slack alerts.

## 3. Feedback & Suggestions for Improvement

To help future FCAJ cohorts become even more refined and deliver a better experience for students, I would like to offer a few small suggestions:

> [!TIP]
> ### 💡 Optimizing the Onboarding Week
> The volume of knowledge, documentation, and processes in the first week is enormous (especially for new students).
> **Suggestion:** Build a detailed **Reading Roadmap** broken down by day, or a specific checklist for the first week. This would help interns allocate their time reasonably, avoid feeling "overwhelmed," and get up to speed on the project faster.

> [!TIP]
> ### 💡 Adding Mock Interview & CV Review Sessions
> Technical skills are honed very well, but the skill of presenting oneself is also extremely important.
> **Suggestion:** The organizers could add Mock Interview sessions or 1-on-1 CV Reviews from the perspective of a recruiter/Senior Engineer toward the end of the internship, helping students feel more confident as they step into the job market.
